## 4. Content gaps vs. the full competitor set (cross-cutting)

1. **No competitor publishes real production capacity, MOQ, or lead times.** Publishing yours (even directionally, e.g. "typical dispatch in X–Y business days for orders above Z pieces") is a differentiator and a featured-snippet target for "school uniform manufacturer lead time."
2. **No competitor has size-recommendation tooling.** Your AI Sizing Assistant is a genuine UGC/engagement signal generator — write a dedicated explainer section about it for the featured-snippet-shaped query "how to choose the right school uniform size."
3. **No competitor structures content by fabric technology** (aero-knit, Teflon stain-repellent, CoolMesh). These are real product attributes you already have in `uniforms.ts` — turn them into an on-page "Fabric & Technology" comparison table, which is highly reusable for People-Also-Ask style queries ("what fabric is used for school sports uniforms").
4. **Nobody targets the corporate/institutional angle** despite your real IndiaMART history showing you already serve corporates, automobile industries, and event management. A `/corporate-uniforms` page is a strong Phase 2 addition once the core 9 pages are live (not included in this package's page count — flagged here as a backlog item).

## 5. Voice search & AI search (SGE/AI Overviews) targeting

Structure content to directly answer spoken/conversational queries:
- "Who makes school uniforms in Tirupur with custom embroidery?"
- "What is the best fabric for school sports uniforms?"
- "How do I get a bulk quote for school uniforms in India?"

Each of these maps to a direct-answer paragraph (40–60 words) placed
immediately under the relevant H2, formatted as a direct declarative answer
before any elaboration — this is what both classic Featured Snippets and
AI Overviews extract from. Implemented in each `metadata/*.md` file under
"Featured Snippet Answer."
